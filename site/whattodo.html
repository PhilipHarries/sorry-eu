Is there anything that can be done to stop Brexit?

What can we do?

"But the public have voted - that's the end of it"

It's important to realise that a referendum is a large and expensive undertaking, that there is no reason to believe that the results were miscounted, and so it has to be accepted that a majority (by over a million people) of voters in the referendum voted to leave the EU. However, there are a number of reasons to believe that people were not fully informed, and that a second referendum would yield a substantially different result.

The reasons for believing there might be a different result are:

  • Lies from the Leave Campaign
    • The NHS will not get £350 million a week after Brexit
    • Britain doesn't contribute £350 a week to the EU
    • The EU dictates policy to Britain against its will
    • Leaving the EU will help control immigration - less of an outright lie, more a failure to correct a widely held assumption
    • Writing in the Telegraph two days after the result, Boris Johnson essentially argues that a new deal with Europe should be incredibly similar to being part of the EU
      • "intense and intensifying European cooperation and partnership in a huge number of fields: the arts, the sciences, the universities, and on improving the environment"
      • "EU citizens living in this country will have their rights fully protected, and the same goes for British citizens living in the EU"
      • "British people will still be able to go and work in the EU; to live; to travel; to study; to buy homes and to settle down"
      • "The only change [...] is that the UK will extricate itself from the EU's [...] system of legislation" (i.e. the European Court of Justice)
    • And it's not just the campaigns alone, there are serious questions to be asked about the media's role in informing the public about the EU and Brexit's consequences
  • Realisation of the impact
    • Economic impact: FTSE and pound fall
    • Regions to lose EU funding
    • Economic Impact: Britain to lose credit rating
  • A widespread misunderstanding of EU realities
  • Scotland likely to leave the UK
  • Impact on Ireland?
  • "Regrexit" and protest votes
    • Britons have taken to Internet Media outlets, Social Media and Traditional Media to voice regret ("Regrexit") over their vote
    • Over three million Britons have signed a petition asking for a second referendum
    • Many votes may have been 'protest votes' by people who didn't think there was a chance the vote would go the way it did - this may have been compounded by people being used to a first-past-the-post system that renders most of the votes cast in general elections as protest votes or foregone conclusions

So get on with it - what can we do?

  • Contact your MP
    • It doesn't matter whether your MP is pro Brexit or against - they represent you - certainly it may feel incredibly unlikely that they'll pay any attention to what you have to say, but it is their job!
    • Let them know what you think, and how you would like them to represent you in Parliament. If it's just you contacting them, they probably won't do anything, it's true - but if enough people register their opinions, it will have an impact.
    • If you're struggling to know what to write feel free to copy and edit my email.
    • If you don't agree with the idea of a second referendum and you're not alone - but still don't like the result, you can still contact your MP and ask that they vote against the government when they attempt to take Britain out of the EU.
  • Register your opinion with the media
    • Write to your paper of choice's letters page, regional and national
    • Post on social media (twitter, facebook)
    • Call in to radio and tv debates
  • Attend a march
  • Convince a friend
  • Tell your MP that you would like them to block Brexit in the Houses of Parliament
    • MP David Lammy is backing a vote in Parliament to halt Brexit and call a second referendum
  • Tell your MSP that you would like them to vote to block Brexit in the Scottish Parliament

And hey, don't lose hope: has article 50 been invoked yet?
